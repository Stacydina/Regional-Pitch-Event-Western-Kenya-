Join us as we bring the community together to support girls participating in Technovation. See local teams of girls showcase and pitch the apps they built to solve community problems. Regional Pitch Event winners will advance on to Technovation global semifinals.

The entire Western Region Technovation team , LakeHub and Safaricom, thanks the Heads of schools that allowed this programme to run in their schools and the mentors/coaches that dedicated themselves to make the girls better.

Meet our judges for the day

Vivian is a software engineer passionate about technology, women and community development. She studied computer science at the University of Nairobi and then proceeded to work at a local startup, mSurvey, as a software engineer. Vivian hasserved as team leader in several youth led organizations including the Google Student Ambassador Programme and WTM Nairobi chapter. She is a recipient of the Google Anita Borg Scholarship and has been a local champion in empowering more women in STEM.Currently, Vivian works as the Google developer community manager.

During her free time she enjoys swimming, coding, travelling, mentoring high school students and volunteering for causes that she believes in.

Mercy Orangi Developer Relations Manager, Andela

Mercy is an experienced tech community specialist in the developer ecosystem in Sub-Saharan Africa, currently the Developer Relations Manager at Andela, and previously at Google as a Developer Ecosystem Community Manager; with a track experience in planning, execution and follow up of tech events, outreach programs, projects and developer bootcamps across the region.

She is passionate about women in tech, with a keen interest in mentorship and information empowerment to increase diversity and inclusion while unearthing top tech talent in the tech industry.

Mercy was also previously an Android developer, and holds a Master of Science (MSc) in Mobile Telecommunications and Innovation, and a Bachelors in Computer Science.

She is an outdoors junkie with a passion for outdoor activities such as hiking and adrenaline sports such as white water rafting.

Roina Ochieng is a 22 year old woman in tech based in Nairobi, Kenya. A graduate of Computer Science and Informatics in Strathmore University, she’s a programmer with experience with developer communities.

She’s the lead developer of the Nairobi chapter of the Google Developer Group and Google Women TechMakers. With massive exposure to the local start-up dynamics in Kenya and Eastern Africa. Her stack is a cocktail of everything Android and Angular JS, Ruby, Python, ASP .NET. Having worked with startups for years now, she is passionate about the power of collaboration to change communities.

She is currently working with the leading Pan-African developer company, Africa’s Talking as a developer success advocate where she oversees how the company supports African developer ecosystem through innovative connective technology.

Margaret Ondeng’ is a Passionate Software Engineer currently pursuing her Masters Degree in Computational Intelligence. She currently works as a Senior Systems Developer at Inmobia Mobile Technology. She has previously also worked with Mahindra Comviva as a Mobile Money Integration Engineer.

Margaret is also the Pitch Africa Lead Coordinator at Startup Africa. Pitch Africa is a an innovative entrepreneurship competition for students and youths that provides them the opportunity to pursue their passions through entrepreneurship

Her passion for tech has seen her win several awards including IBM Master the Mainframe Championship in New York, and Facebook Grace Hopper Scholarship in Texas.

Margaret is a dynamic speaker and has featured on various platforms talking about her work in technology and mentorship.

Esther has more than four years of experience in the nonprofit. She is a tech enthusiast, an entrepreneur, and a speaker.

She is one of the founders of Sokonect, a platform that links small scale farmers to buyers at the comfort of their farms using mobile technology. Through Sokonect she drives partnerships for tackling social problems through connectivity and appropriate technology. She currently works at the American Red Cross in Washington DC as a technical Advisor, preparedness ICTs.

She also serves as a board member at Girls in Tech Kenyan Chapter, a non-profit organization that seeks to increase women visibility in the technology space through workshops, training and conferences. She has volunteered for Technovation as a coach, mentor and judge.
